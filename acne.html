Acne

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It can manifest as pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, and cysts.

Causes

  • Hormonal changes (especially during puberty, menstruation, and pregnancy).
  • Overproduction of sebum (oil) by sebaceous glands.
  • Clogged hair follicles due to dead skin cells.
  • Bacterial infection by Propionibacterium acnes.
  • Diet

Symptoms

  • Pimples with whiteheads or blackheads.
  • Redness and inflammation around pimples.
  • Pustules (pus-filled pimples).
  • Cysts (deep, painful lesions).
  • Scars if not treated

Treatment

Treatment for acne can include over-the-counter medications (benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid), prescription medications (retinoids, antibiotics), and in-office procedures like chemical peels and laser therapy. A consistent skincare routine, and avoiding harsh chemicals or over-exfoliating can also help improve your acne condition.
