Ringworm

Ringworm, also known as dermatophytosis, is a common fungal infection. Despite its name, it is caused by a fungus, not a worm, and is characterized by a ring-shaped rash.

Causes

  • Fungal infection, typically by Trichophyton or Microsporum species.
  • Contact with infected people or animals.
  • Contact with contaminated objects or surfaces

Symptoms

  • Ring-shaped rash with raised, scaly borders
  • Itching
  • Redness or discoloration

Treatment

Ringworm is usually treated with antifungal creams, ointments, or sprays that can be purchased over-the-counter. For severe cases, prescription-strength antifungals may be required
